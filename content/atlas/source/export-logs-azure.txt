.. _export-logs-azure:

=====================================
Export Logs to {+az-bs+}
=====================================

.. meta::
   :description: Export logs from `mongod`, `mongos`, and audit logs to an Azure Blob Storage from an `M10+` cluster every minute using Atlas.

.. default-domain:: mongodb

.. facet::
   :name: genre
   :values: tutorial

.. contents:: On this page
   :local:
   :backlinks: none
   :depth: 1
   :class: singlecol

You can configure your ``M10+`` {+service+} clusters to export system 
logs every minute to an {+az-bs+} container.

This integration allows you to:

- Specify which MongoDB log files you want to export to the 
  {+az-bs+} container. {+service+} supports exporting the following log types:

  - ``mongod``
  - ``mongos``
  - ``mongod-audit``
  - ``mongos-audit``

- Set up to 10 export paths, allowing you to export logs to multiple 
  {+az-bs+} containers simultaneously.

.. important::
   
   Logs can contain sensitive information (including PII). You are 
   responsible for the storage and treatment of your logs in your 
   {+az-bs+} container. To have 
   {+service+} :ref:`redact certain information 
   <database-log-redaction>` before exporting 
   logs, contact |mdb-support|.

Required Access
---------------

To export logs to an {+az-bs+} container, you must have 
:authrole:`Project Owner` or :authrole:`Organization Owner` access to 
|service|.

Considerations
--------------

- Each {+service+} host typically produces 1 GB of logs per day. 
  Exporting logs incurs a data transfer cost. The exact data transfer 
  cost varies depending on the destination, region, and cloud 
  provider.          
- Network issues or retries can cause duplicate log
  entries in your {+az-bs+} container.

Prerequisites
-------------

You'll need:

- An existing {+az-bs+} container.
- Permissions to allow |service| to write to the container (for 
  example, with an SAS token or Azure AD authorization).
- An Azure Service Principal authorized for |service| access.
- An ``M10+`` |service| cluster running MongoDB 7.0 or later.

Procedure
---------

To export logs to an {+az-bs+} container, complete the 
following steps.

.. procedure:: 
   :style: normal

   .. include:: /includes/nav/steps-project-integrations.rst

   .. step:: Click :guilabel:`Configure` on the :guilabel:`Azure Blob Storage Log Monitoring` card.

   .. step:: Grant {+service+} access to your |azure| account.

      a. From the :guilabel:`Service Principal` dropdown, select the 
         Azure Service Principal for |service| to write logs to your 
         Azure Blob Storage container. 
      
         To add a Service Principal, see :ref:`manage-azure-access`.

      #. Click :guilabel:`Next`.

   .. step:: Connect {+service+} to your {+az-bs+} container. 

      a. In the :guilabel:`Storage Account Name` field, enter the name 
         of your {+az-bs+} account as it appears in your |azure| 
         account.

      #. In the :guilabel:`Container Name` field, enter the name of 
         your {+az-bs+} container.

      #. In the :guilabel:`Prefix path` field, enter a directory name 
         to organize the contents of your {+az-bs+} container. For 
         example, entering ``logs/`` creates a 
         ``logs`` directory in your {+az-bs+} container to store 
         the exported logs. 

      #. Under :guilabel:`Log Type`, select the types of logs you want to export.

         .. include:: /includes/fact-atlas-log-types.rst

      #. Click :guilabel:`Next`. 

   .. step:: Assign an access policy to your |azure| |iam| role.

      a. Follow the Azure Portal or Atlas CLI steps outlined in the 
         {+atlas-ui+}.         

      #. Click :guilabel:`Validate` to confirm your configuration and 
         credentials are correct before enabling the export.

   .. step:: Click :guilabel:`Done`.
