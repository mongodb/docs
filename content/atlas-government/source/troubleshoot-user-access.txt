===============================
Troubleshoot User Access Issues
===============================

.. meta::
   :description: Learn how to troubleshoot issues with MongoDB Atlas for Government user accounts.

.. default-domain:: mongodb

.. contents:: On this page
   :local:
   :backlinks: none
   :depth: 1
   :class: singlecol

.. note::

   This page covers |cloudgov| user accounts. |cloudgov-short| users 
   are separate from database users. Database users can access MongoDB 
   databases, while |cloudgov-short| users can access the 
   |cloudgov-short| application itself.

One-Time Password and MFA Migration Flow
----------------------------------------

As part of the transition of |cloudgov-short| to an improved 
authentication environment, password users must go through a one-time 
self-service migration when they log in on or after March 18, 
2026.

.. important:: Migration deadline

   To ensure a smooth migration to the new authentication environment:

   - You must log in to |cloudgov-short| and complete the one-time 
     MFA migration flow by June 30, 2026.

   - Users who do not log in by this date might require a full account 
     recovery before they can access |cloudgov-short| again.

   If you are an organization owner, we recommend that you notify 
   impacted users and ensure they log in before the migration deadline.

To complete the one-time password and MFA migration flow:

.. procedure::
   :style: normal
   
   .. step:: Sign in with your existing credentials.

      a. Go to the |cloudgov-short| login page.

      #. Specify your existing username and password.

   .. step:: Complete a one-time MFA challenge with your existing factors.

      AtlasGov prompts you to verify your identity using your existing 
      MFA factors (for example, a security key, biometric factor, or 
      TOTP code).
      
      This page will look slightly different from your usual MFA 
      screen, but it uses your current factors for verification.

   .. step:: Enroll MFA in the new environment.

      After you successfully verify your account with your existing 
      factor, |cloudgov-short| prompts you to configure at least one 
      more authentication factor in the new environment. Depending on 
      your organization's policies, you might register one or more of the following authentication factors:

      - Security key or biometric factor (WebAuthn).
      - Time-based one-time password (TOTP) authenticator app.
      - Add additional factors your organization allows.

   .. step:: Log in normally.

      After you complete this one-time migration flow, future logins 
      use the new MFA configuration and follow your usual login 
      experience.

Troubleshoot Password and MFA Migration
---------------------------------------

Use the following guidance if you or another user encounters issues 
during the one-time password and MFA migration.

I can't verify my existing security key or biometric factor
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- If you have another MFA factor configured (for example, a TOTP 
  authenticator app), approve the challenge using that factor.


- If you do not have any alternate factor configured, do one of the 
  following steps:

  - Contact your organization owner so they can open a case with 
    MongoDB Support through the |cloudgov-short| Support Portal.

  - If you are an organization owner, open a Support case and include 
    affected usernames and organization details.

MongoDB Support can help you recover access and re-enroll MFA factors 
as needed. To learn more, see :ref:`support`.

I forgot my password and still can't log in after resetting it
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

If you reset your password, but still can't complete the migration or 
log in:

.. procedure::
   :style: normal

   .. step:: Confirm you reset the password from |cloudgov-short|.

      Confirm that you reset the password from the |cloudgov-short| 
      login page (not from commercial |service|).

   .. step:: Try the migration flow again with your new password.

   .. step:: If needed, request support.

      If you continue to experience issues (for example, loops between 
      password reset and MFA prompts, do one of the following steps:

      - Contact your organization owner.

      - Open a case with MongoDB Support from the |cloudgov-short| 
        Support Portal. To learn more, see :ref:`support`.

I lost access to all of my MFA factors
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

If you no longer have access to any of your configured MFA factors (for 
example, you lost your security key or no longer have access to the 
authenticator app):

.. procedure::
   :style: normal

   .. step:: Contact your organization owner or MongoDB Support.

      To learn more, see :ref:`support`.

   .. step:: Verify your identity. 
    
      Verify your identity according to your organization's internal
      process.

   .. step:: Enroll new authentication factors.

      After you verify your identity, MongoDB Support can help reset
      your MFA configuration so that you can enroll new factors in the 
      new environment.

I did not complete the migration before the deadline
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

If you do not log in to |cloudgov-short| and complete the migration 
flow before June 30, 2026, your account might require a full account 
recovery:

- Organization owners should open a case with MongoDB Support and 
  provide a list of impacted users.

- MongoDB Support will guide you through the recovery process for both 
  password and federated accounts that did not complete migration 
  before the deadline.

To learn more, see :ref:`support`.
