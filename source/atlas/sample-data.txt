================
Load Sample Data
================

.. default-domain:: mongodb

.. contents:: On this page
   :local:
   :backlinks: none
   :depth: 2
   :class: singlecol

Overview
--------

.. short-description::
   
   Load our sample data into your cluster to quickly get started 
   experimenting with data in MongoDB.

In this guide, you will load our sample data into your cluster.

.. time:: 5

What You'll Need
----------------

- A MongoDB account. See :ref:`guides-create-atlas-account`.
- An Atlas cluster. See :ref:`guides-create-a-cluster`.

Procedure
---------

.. procedure::
   :style: normal

   .. step:: Navigate to the Database Deployments page for your project.

      Navigate to the {+db-deployments+} page for your project.

   .. step:: Click on your cluster name.

   .. step:: Click the Collections tab.
      
   .. step:: Click Load Sample Dataset.
      
   .. step:: In the ensuing dialog, click Load Sample Dataset confirm.

      Once the load completes, the :guilabel:`Collections` tab refreshes 
      to show your sample data.

      You should see the following databases in your cluster:

      .. list-table::
         :header-rows: 1
         :widths: 20 30

         * - Database Name
           - Description

         * - ``sample-airbnb``
           -  Contains details on `AirBnB <https://www.airbnb.com>`__ 
              listings.

         * - ``sample-analytics``
           -  Contains training data for a mock financial services
              application.

         * - ``sample-geospatial``
           - Contains shipwreck data.

         * - ``sample-mflix``
           - Contains movie data.

         * - ``sample-restaurants``
           - Contains restaurant data.

         * - ``sample-supplies``
           - Contains data from a mock office supply store.

         * - ``sample-training``
           - Contains MongoDB training services dataset.

         * - ``sample-weather``
           - Contains detailed weather reports.

Summary
-------

If you successfully completed the procedure in this guide, you have 
loaded our sample data. 

In the next guide, you will add a database user.
