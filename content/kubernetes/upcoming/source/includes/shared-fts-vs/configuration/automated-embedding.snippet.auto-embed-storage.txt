
``mongot`` persists embeddings in a dedicated internal database
on the cluster. ``mongot`` does not store embeddings in your
source collection. Storing embeddings separately keeps your
collection clean and keeps embedding storage out of your
operational disk budget for application data.

- You cannot directly query the embedding vectors in the internal
  database. ``mongot`` manages the embeddings.

- Your source collection backup does not include the embeddings.
  ``mongot`` can regenerate embeddings from the source text, and
  you incur charges for embedding generation.

- Changes to the embedding model, output dimensions, or
  quantization trigger a full re-embedding of the affected
  index, for which you incur charges for embedding generation.
  See :ref:`mongot-auto-embed-regeneration`.

To learn more, see :ref:`auto-embed-materialized-views`.
