=======================
Reset Connector Offsets
=======================

.. meta::
   :description: Learn how to reset the offsets for your MongoDB Kafka connector by modifying the associated Kafka consumer group offsets using command-line tools.

.. contents:: On this page
   :local:
   :backlinks: none
   :depth: 2
   :class: singlecol


Overview
--------

You can manually change the position in a {+kafka+} topic partition from
which a consumer group processes messages by resetting the offset. A reset
typically moves the offset to an earlier position, such as the beginning of
a topic, so that the connector reprocesses messages from that point forward.
You can also move the offset to a later position to skip messages.

Resetting a Source Connector Offset 
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The {+source-connector+} provides a way to reset offsets by using
the ``offset.partition.name`` configuration property. To learn more,
see :ref:`Reset Stored Offsets <troubleshoot-reset-stored-offsets>`.

Resetting a Sink Connector Offset
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

MongoDB does not support resetting offsets for the {+sink-connector+}.
However, you can reset the offsets of the consumer group associated with your
sink connector by using the {+kafka+} consumer group tool.

To learn more, see the
`Kafka consumer group tool <https://docs.confluent.io/platform/current/clients/consumer.html#ak-consumer-group-tool>`__
page in the Confluent documentation.

Considerations
--------------

Before you reset your {+source-connector+} or {+sink-connector+} offsets,
consider the following:

- Resetting offsets can result in data inconsistency or data loss, as 
  messages can be skipped or reprocessed. Use this procedure carefully.
- The commands in this procedure operate on {+kafka+} only, not on
  your MongoDB deployment. Required steps may vary between
  providers and configurations. Review your Kafka provider's
  documentation and confirm with your provider or administrator
  that this procedure is appropriate for your environment.
